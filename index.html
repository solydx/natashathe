About Me

Hi, I’m Natasha — a maker, builder, economist-in-training, and multipotentialite who thrives at the intersection of craft and technology. My journey spans woodworking, CNC machining, upholstery, CAD/CAM modeling, and economics. On TikTok and other platforms, I share my process: from shaping Baltic birch into modular pet steps to experimenting with laser-cutting, welding, and upholstery techniques.

My work lives under multiple brands — Groondlings for modular furniture and upholstery design, and Solyd Solutions for open-source maker tech, CNC files, and digital systems. I believe in empowering communities through design, Industry 4.0/5.0 tools, and resilient local manufacturing.

At heart, I’m not only a maker of things but also a builder of systems and futures. Whether in the classroom studying economics or in the workshop clamping, routing, and coding toolpaths, my mission is the same: to create, connect, and inspire others to realize their potential.
